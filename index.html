The problem ZTR solves

In Miller v. Regions Bank (N.D. Alabama, 2026), a US federal court applied a two-track doctrine: the AI error received a public reprimand. The destruction of the ChatGPT account — the absence of any contemporaneous record — received adverse inference, contempt, and a six-month suspension.

The same pattern appeared in New York, in California (H.C. v. Contreras), and in the UK (CPS/Tobosaru, EWHC 2026). Four jurisdictions. One common thread: the system penalises the absence of a record more severely than the error itself.

All cases documented at primary source level (PACER, BAILII, courts.ca.gov). L₂ research framework — zorthex.com

How it works

01

You review the document

You use Claude to draft or research a document. You apply your professional judgment.

02

ZTR timestamps the review

Ask Claude to verify with ZTR. The document is hashed (SHA-256) and an eIDAS-qualified timestamp is recorded. Document never stored.

03

You receive a receipt

A dated, immutable record: ZTR-20260720143022-a7f3c891. If ever asked whether you verified — you can prove it.

Document never stored

The document is hashed and immediately discarded. Only 64 characters (the SHA-256 hash) are retained. Your content stays private.

eIDAS-qualified timestamps

Timestamps issued by Aruba PEC S.p.A., a Qualified Trust Service Provider under eIDAS. Legally presumed accurate across the EU and UK. RFC 3161 compliant.

Audit trail built-in

Every verification is stored with a receipt ID. Retrieve any past receipt instantly. Export your full verification history for compliance review.

Works inside Claude

ZTR is a Claude connector. No new software to install. No new workflow to learn. One command before filing.

What ZTR does NOT do

  • Does not verify document content or detect errors
  • Does not detect AI hallucinations (that requires on-premise tools)
  • Does not replace professional judgment
  • Does not guarantee compliance with any specific rule or standard
  • Does not store, read, or transmit the original document
